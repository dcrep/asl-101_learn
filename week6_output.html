More relevant words to know:

  • STUPID

    • DO-DO Meanings:

    Depending on context:

    • What are you doing?
    • What did you do?
    • What did they do?
    • What are they doing?
    • What are you going to do?
    • What should I do?
    • What will you do?
    (DO-DO translation is not "to do"; ACTIVITY is closer to "to do")

    FOR-FOR Meanings:

    Depending on context:

    • What for?
    • For what reason?
    • What did you do that for?
    • What did they do that for?
    • For what?

    Practice:

    What did you do yesterday?   [YESTERDAY, DO-DO?]
    What do you do on days off?   [NONE WORK, DO-DO?]
    What are you doing in English class?   [ENGLISH CLASS, DO-DO?]

    ACTIVITY closely translates to "to do":

    I am doing my best   [ME ACTIVITY MY BEST]
    I have nothing to do today   [TODAY, NONE ACTIVITY]
    I have something to do   [HAVE SOMETHING ACTIVITY]

    Do you like coffee?   [YOU LIKE COFFEE?]
    Did you forget something?   [YOU FORGET SOMETHING?]
    Do you need help?   [NEED ME-HELP-YOU?]

    I don't have to go to class today   [TODAY NOT MUST GO-TO CLASS] \ [^CLASS TODAY^, NOT MUST GO-TO]
    I do not want that   [NOT-WANT THAT] \ [^THAT^, NOT-WANT]

    I wanted to show you something yesterday
    Could you let me know where the bookstore is?
    Hurry up! I have to go to class!
    Can you bring me the laptop please?
    Do you want to go with me to class?
    That class has 22 students
    What happened in English class?
    I had class earlier today

    Signer A: HOW YOU? YOU GO-TO LIBRARY WHEN?
    Signer B: LATER. NOW ME GO-TO CAFETERIA. COME-BACK SOON
    A: OH-I-SEE. BRING-HERE FOOD CAN?
    B: SURE. HAVE CLASS TODAY?
    A: YES! ME TAKE-UP FRENCH, ENGLISH, MATH
    B: COOL. ME SPEAK FRENCH. NEED ME-HELP-YOU PRACTICE?
    A: YES PLEASE! THANK-YOU!
    B: HAPPY HELP-YOU. LATER YOU-SHOW-ME YOUR BOOK ME LOOK-AT
    A: SEE-YOU SOON

    Signer A: EXCUSE-ME. WHAT-WRONG?
    Signer B: SORRY, SHOULD ASK-YOU. THEATER WHERE? HAVE CLASS SOON
    A: UNDERSTAND. GO-TO LIBRARY, THEATER THERE
    B: THANKS-A-LOT. NEED HELP-YOU?
    A: NO, ME FINE. NOW WRITE PAPER.
    B: FOR-FOR? ENGLISH CLASS?
    A: CORRECT. READ BOOK, FINISH, MUST THINK, WRITE PAPER. NOT-LIKE
    B: OH-I-SEE. ME ALREADY TAKE-UP ENGLISH. YOU WILL FINE
    A: THANK-YOU. MUST REMEMBER STUDY
