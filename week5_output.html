    • DO-DO Meanings:

    Depending on context:

    • What are you doing?
    • What did you do?
    • What did they do?
    • What are they doing?
    • What are you going to do?
    • What should I do?
    • What will you do?
    (DO-DO translation is not "to do"; ACTIVITY is closer to "to do")

    FOR-FOR Meanings:

    Depending on context:

    • What for?
    • For what reason?
    • What did you do that for?
    • What did they do that for?
    • For what?

    Practice:

    With a partner, practice + and - the numbers 1-30
    PLUS
    MINUS/NEGATIVE
    EQUAL-TO

    Ex. 12 PLUS 16 EQUAL-TO (eyebrows down)

    How are you?   [HOW YOU?]
    What do you want to learn?   [YOU WANT LEARN WHAT?]
    Do you need paper?   [YOU NEED PAPER?]
    Where do you go to school?   [YOU STUDENT WHERE?]
    How many classes are you taking?   [CLASS YOU TAKE-UP HOW-MANY?]
    Do you need to go to class?   [YOU NEED GO-TO CLASS?]
    Do you live in New York?   [YOU LIVE NEW-YORK?]
    Can you help me?   [CAN YOU-HELP-ME?]
    What classes are you taking?   [CLASS YOU TAKE-UP WHAT?]
    What are you doing later?   [LATER DO-DO?]

    Look at the paper   [PAPER, LOOK-AT]
    I am moving soon   [MOVE SOON ME]
    I have to remember to study   [MUST REMEMBER STUDY]
    I am going to the library today   [TODAY ME GO-TO LIBRARY]
    I went to the library earlier   [PAST ME GO-TO LIBRARY]
    I already went to the library   [ME FINISH GO-TO LIBRARY]
    She explained the cultural perspective well   [SHE EXPLAIN CULTURE PERCEIVE GOOD]
